### Item 1: Transition slide framing

Reject "could be" ΓÇö it signals doubt to the one audience where doubt is most costly. The Critic's concern (forecast vs. aspiration) is real but the fix is wrong. "Will be" is a prediction; "could be" is a hedge. What leaders need is a *target with agency*: **"Where leading groups are positioning themselves."**

This reframe is accurate (it describes a current trend, not a prediction), avoids the forecast problem, and carries the right political charge ΓÇö it implies DTU can lead or fall behind. No credibility risk.

**Proposal:** Replace "Where most groups will be in 12 months" ΓåÆ "Where leading groups are positioning themselves now."

### Item 2: #2b title

Both Realist and Critic titles are defensive ΓÇö "Choices" implies uncertainty, "Policy/Approved" positions DTU as reactive to rules. A leadergroup deck should lead with strategy, not compliance.

**Counter-proposal:** **"Why DTU Builds Its Own AI Agents"**

This is a headline, not a label. It prompts a question the slide then answers. It positions DTU as active and intentional, and the content (in-house vs. cloud distinction) lands naturally as the answer rather than the topic. If this is too bold, a fallback is "DTU's AI Architecture: In-House Agents vs. Cloud Tools" ΓÇö still top-down because "architecture" implies a deliberate design choice.

I reject the Critic's "Policy: What's Approved" framing ΓÇö it tells leaders to think about compliance, when we want them thinking about competitive positioning.

### Item 3: Productivity phrasing

"Floors not ceilings" is punchy and correct ΓÇö the Critic is objecting to rhetorical force, not accuracy. The causal claim (studies used 1-2 year old tools) is precisely what justifies the floor framing. These are not alternatives; the causal claim *is* the argument for using floor language.

**Proposed synthesis:** Keep "published figures are floors, not ceilings" as the header claim, immediately supported by: "Studies published today used agents 1-2 years old ΓÇö a different generation of tools." This is advocacy in the best sense: a defensible claim, clearly argued.

If the forum insists on dropping "floors/ceilings," the minimum acceptable phrasing is: "Published figures likely understate current potential ΓÇö they used tools 1-2 years old, before agent workflows matured." No vaguer than that.

### Item 4: Scope slide binary

Choose B ΓÇö pair the mention with a commitment. Dropping teaching/admin entirely is a missed strategic opportunity. For leaders, the scope slide does two jobs: (1) set expectations for today's talk, and (2) signal that AI at DTU has a broader roadmap they're responsible for.

**Proposed language:** "Today's focus: research. Separate conversations are needed for teaching and admin ΓÇö both are in scope for DTU's AI strategy, neither is simple." One sentence. No deferral, but also no false promise. Leaders hear: "This person has thought beyond today's slide."
